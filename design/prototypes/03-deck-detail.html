"EALA Memory"

E - Epinephrine

How to use: Repeat "EALA" before each shift. Each letter represents the first medication in the ACLS protocol sequence.
Story

"The ER Journey"

Picture yourself in a busy ER. The alarms sound as a code blue is called. You rush to the crash cart and grab Epinephrine first, then reach for Amiodarone as you prepare to stabilize the rhythm...

How to use: Walk through this story visualization before studying. The narrative helps anchor each medication in sequence.
Visual

"Medicine Cabinet Method"

Imagine a medicine cabinet with 4 shelves. Top shelf: Epinephrine (adrenaline rush). Second shelf: Amiodarone (heart rhythm). Third shelf: Lidocaine (local anesthetic). Bottom shelf: Atropine (parasympathetic blocker).

How to use: Visualize opening the cabinet from top to bottom. Each shelf represents one medication in order.
